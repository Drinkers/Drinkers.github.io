Anaglyph image


Principle: Each eye receives a slightly different view of a scene, and this will cause a binocular disparity. The binocular disparity information will help to form depth perception. There are several ways to add depth, here we use the glasses with different lens. There will be no red light that will enter the left eye, and there will be no blue light that will enter right eye. Together, when we put two slightly different picture together, which correspond to the views received by left and right eyes, they will form anaglyph images.
Method: Using Photoshop, we can create anaglyph photos easily. Open up two different layers, and add two pictures that show the scenes received by left or right eye. Close the blue channel for the left eye layer, and close the red channel for the right eye channel. Then save the picture.
